White House says its not responsable for recent missile attacks on Iran

Friday December 2nd 2022

The Biden Administration and its detractors agree that Iran should not have launched a ballistic missile at Erbil in Iraq, but they disagree on the appropriate response. National Security Advisor Jake Sullivan of the White House released a statement on Monday in which he stated that "the United States stands with Prime Minister [Mustafa] Kadhimi and the leaders of the Kurdistan Region, President Nechirvan Barzani and Prime Minister Masrour Barzani, in condemning this assault on the sovereignty of Iraq and its Kurdistan region." "We will back the Government of Iraq in holding Iran accountable, and we will support our partners throughout the Middle East in addressing similar threats from Iran," it said, adding that the US supports "the full sovereignty, independence, and territorial integrity of Iraq." Iran claimed it had a target. Iran claimed it fired missiles at an Israeli espionage base in Erbil, but they also damaged a Kurdish TV station, a residence that was likely frequented by Israelis, and were not far from the US consulate. In an editorial, The Wall Street Journal stated, "With each Iranian provocation, the Biden Administration's relentless pursuit of a nuclear agreement becomes more difficult to comprehend. The most recent occurred on Sunday when missiles were fired near a US Consulate that was being built in northern Iraq." The attack on the US consulate in Erbil, Iraq, has prompted a number of lawmakers and former American officials to call for the termination of the Iran nuclear talks. However, some senior US officials have appeared to suggest that the attack will not have an impact on the likelihood of reaching a nuclear agreement.
